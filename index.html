Fractions

A fraction represents a part of a whole, more generally, any number of equal parts.

Example

This excercise combine multiplication, division and addition:

$ \underbrace{\frac{3}{7}\times\frac{1}{2}} + \underbrace{\frac{2}{21} \div \frac{1}{3}} $

1. First we solve the multiplication and division:

$\require{cancel} \frac{3}{14} \ + \frac{2}{\cancel{21}^{7}} \times \frac{\cancel{3}^{1}}{1} $

$ \frac{3}{14} \ + \frac{2}{7} $

2. Now we solve the addition, preforming the least common multiple:

$ \frac{3}{14}+\frac{2 \times 2}{7 \times 2} = $

$ \frac{3}{13}+\frac{4}{14}=\frac{7}{14} $

$ = \frac{1}{2}\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \textrm{simplify answer} $

Decimal to Fraction

A fraction in which denominator is 10 or it is 102,103 etc. A power of 10 is called decimal fraction, for example, 3/10, 3/100 they can be represented as 0.3, 0.03 etc.

Example

Here is the number written as a decimal:

0,12

1. Place the decinal part over a denominator equal to its place value:

$ \Rightarrow 0,12 = \frac{12}{100} $

Decimal to percent

A percent is a part of a whole out of 100. For example, 20 % means 20/100 (we can say "20 parts from 100"). This can also be written as 0.2.

Example

Look at this decimal number:

0,14

1. Move the decimal point two places to the right or multiply by 100

$ \Rightarrow 0,\underrightarrow{1}\underrightarrow{4} = 14\% $

$ 0,14 \times 100 = 14 \% $

Percent to decimal

The inverse operation of decimal to percent.

Example

Transform the percent representation to decimal:

51%

1. Move the decimal point to places to the left or divide by 100

$ \underleftarrow{5}\underleftarrow{1}\% = 0,51 $

or

$ \frac{51}{100} = 0,51 $

Percent to fraction

Using the relation with 100. We can turn a percent in a fraction using division operation.

Example

Represent the percent number as a fraction:

36%

1. Divide by 100

$ \Rightarrow \frac{36}{100} $

2. Reduce

$ \frac{36}{100} = \frac{9}{25} $

Fraction to decimal

Divide the numerator into the denominator.

Example

Write the next number in decimal form:

$\frac{2}{5}$

\( \Rightarrow 2 |\underline{5} \\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ 0,4\)

Fraction to percentage

Divide the numerator into the denominator. Then, move the decimal point two places to the right to convert the decimal into a percentage.

Example

Represent the next number as a percentage:

$\frac{1}{10}$

1. Divide the numerator into the denominator

\( \Rightarrow 1 |\underline{10} \Rightarrow \frac{1}{10} = 0,1 \\ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ \ 0,1 \) decimal

2. Move the decimal point two place to the right (convert the decimal into a percentage)

$ 0,1 = 10\% $
